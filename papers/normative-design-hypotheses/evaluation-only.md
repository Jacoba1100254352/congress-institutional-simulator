# Normative Design Hypotheses Evaluation

Status: do not draft this paper now.

## Should This Paper Exist?

Not yet.

A future normative institutional-design paper could exist, but only after the empirical-validation, robustness/failure-mode, political-science, and chamber-structure work is stronger. The current simulator can generate design hypotheses, not adoption recommendations.

The safest current role for this idea is a short future-work note: portfolio safeguards, content-selection stages, harm review, anti-capture access, and reversibility are mechanisms worth further study under stronger empirical and adversarial tests.

## Evidence Required Before Drafting

Required before reconsidering a normative paper:

- stronger empirical boundary work that separates validated, sanity-checkable, proxy-only, synthetic-only, and unmodeled claims;
- district-level public opinion or credible representation proxies;
- lobbying-to-bill and campaign-finance linkage;
- implementation, law revision, court review, repeal, sunset, and reauthorization data where correction/reversibility is discussed;
- adversarial robustness experiments with explicit actors, budgets, information levels, attack success rates, and worst-case degradation;
- benchmark fairness controls where conventional and simple-majority baselines receive comparable information, amendment, review, and attention budgets;
- multidimensional policy-space sensitivity;
- representation diagnostics that distinguish public support, district support, affected-group support, and chamber coalition support;
- value-profile sensitivity showing how conclusions change under different normative weights;
- legal and administrative feasibility analysis that is external to the simulator;
- clear distinction between design hypotheses, empirical evidence, and normative commitments.

## Safe Claims

Safe claims now:

- The simulator can generate design hypotheses under explicit synthetic assumptions.
- Portfolio safeguards, content selection, harm review, anti-capture access, and reversibility are mechanism families worth further study.
- The current results suggest that different mechanisms separate productivity, support, risk, moderation, and administrative burden in the implemented synthetic worlds.
- Content-selection stages and review/routing safeguards should be tested under stronger fairness controls, generator sensitivities, and adversarial conditions.
- Any normative interpretation depends on value choices that the simulator cannot settle.
- Current empirical materials are benchmark and sanity-check supports, not validation of institutional superiority.

Safe future claims, if required evidence is added:

- Under specified empirical boundaries and adversarial assumptions, some mechanism families appear more robust than others on specified diagnostics.
- Certain safeguards create measurable risk/cost tradeoffs under defined value profiles.
- Particular institutional ideas deserve legal, administrative, and field-specific feasibility study.

## Unsafe Claims

Unsafe claims now:

- The simulator proves that portfolio safeguards, content selection, harm review, anti-capture access, or reversibility should be adopted.
- The simulator identifies a generally superior legislative design.
- Synthetic welfare results are empirical evidence of real-world institutional superiority.
- Generated public support is observed public opinion.
- Generated public benefit is social welfare.
- Lobbying/capture diagnostics validate real corruption or capture effects.
- Harm metrics validate real minority-rights effects.
- Administrative-cost metrics validate real implementation cost.
- A compromise-weighted or other scalar value profile settles normative priorities.
- A portfolio hybrid should be presented as the preferred design candidate.
- A normative paper can proceed before robustness and validation work.

## Should It Wait?

Yes.

This paper should wait until after:

1. the empirical-validation/data roadmap has been implemented beyond flow sanity checks;
2. the robustness/failure-mode paper has explicit adversary experiments and worst-case reporting;
3. the political-science paper has stronger benchmark fairness controls;
4. the chamber-structure paper has representation diagnostics and validation targets;
5. the software/artifact package has clean reproducibility and packaging metadata if the normative paper relies on public reuse.

Current recommendation: do not create a normative manuscript. Preserve this as an evaluation-only gate.
