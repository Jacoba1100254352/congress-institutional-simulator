# Related Work Targets

This is a target list for later citation work, not a finished bibliography. Verify each source before citing.

## Political Theory Frame

- Spatial voting and status-quo-relative utility.
- Pivotal politics and gridlock intervals.
- Veto-player theory.
- Committee gatekeeping and committee information theories.
- Agenda control, majority cartel, floor scheduling, and discharge petitions.
- Bicameralism, executive vetoes, and override politics.
- Legislative effectiveness and productivity measurement.

## Lobbying and Influence

- Lobbying as information.
- Lobbying as access.
- Lobbying as legislative subsidy.
- Campaign finance and organized interest influence.
- Capture, transparency, and audit mechanisms.

## Representation and Public Support

- Public opinion and policy congruence.
- District-level opinion estimation.
- Affected-group representation.
- Public mandate and low-support enactment concepts.

## Empirical Data Sources to Verify

- Congress.gov and govinfo bill status.
- Voteview roll calls and party-unity data.
- LDA lobbying disclosure data.
- FEC/OpenFEC campaign finance.
- Committee hearing, referral, markup, and amendment data.
- Supreme Court Database and invalidation datasets.
- Federal Register, Regulations.gov, Unified Agenda, and agency enforcement data.
- OLRC/statutory lineage and law revision data.
- ParlGov, V-Dem, IPU/chamber data, and cross-national legislative productivity sources.

## Methods and Simulation

- Agent-based legislative simulation.
- Model calibration versus validation.
- Sensitivity analysis and uncertainty decomposition.
- Paired simulation comparisons and common-random-number designs.

## Citation Rules

- Do not use a source to support a claim unless it has been checked.
- Do not cite coalition size or party unity as public opinion.
- Do not cite lobbying spend observability as capture validation.
- Keep the ACM framework paper out of the theory contribution except as a methods/artifact reference.
