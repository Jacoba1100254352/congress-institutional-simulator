# Related Work Targets

This file lists citation targets to verify before drafting. It is not a bibliography and does not assert that the current repository already contains all references.

## Core Political Science and Public Choice

- Spatial voting and ideal-point models.
- Agenda control and amendment sequencing.
- Veto-player theory and bicameralism.
- Legislative bargaining and party discipline.
- Committee gatekeeping, specialization, and information.
- Lobbying as information, pressure, and access.
- Legislative productivity and gridlock measurement.

## Computational and Simulation Methods

- Agent-based models of legislatures.
- Social simulation validation and calibration.
- ODD and ODD+D documentation standards.
- Sensitivity analysis under generator uncertainty.
- Robustness, uncertainty, and simulation result presentation.

## Empirical Anchors

- Voteview roll-call and ideal-point measures.
- Congress.gov bill progression and sponsor data.
- LDA lobbying-disclosure data.
- Comparative Agendas Project topic coding.
- Measures of legislative effectiveness/productivity.
- Public-opinion and district-level representation data.

## Citation Rules for the Draft

- Do not cite a source for a claim unless the source has been opened and checked.
- Do not use empirical legislative data sources to validate synthetic public benefit, harm, or capture unless the mapping is explicit.
- Keep software-framework citations in the methods section; the political-science paper should not re-present the ACM contribution as its novelty.
